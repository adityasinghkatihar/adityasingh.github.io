About

Aditya Singh used to spend his time convincing proteins to behave. Now he spends even more time trying to convince data to make sense.

He is a biologist by training and a generalist by instinct — most comfortable in the space between disciplines that don't normally talk to each other. His background is in host–pathogen biology and metabolic flux reprogramming, but his attention keeps drifting toward the methods: how experiments are designed, how data is interpreted, and whether the tools being used are actually the right ones for the question being asked.

He has a habit of noticing patterns before he can fully explain them. This is sometimes useful. Mostly annoying.

His working philosophy is simple: better methods create better questions. He is currently interested in how computational and automated machine learning approaches can be folded into biological research — not to replace intuition, but to make it more reliable. He does not claim to have everything figured out.

Outside the lab, he is usually somewhere between curiosity, overthinking, and a stubborn belief that research should eventually become simpler than the person doing it.

Protease-mediated regulation of metabolic flux in Candida glabrata

How does a GPI-anchored aspartyl protease regulate metabolic adaptation under host-induced stress?

Detail↓
  • Established interaction between CgYps1 and key metabolic enzymes in glycolysis and the pentose phosphate pathway.
  • Purified catalytically active CgYps1 via Pichia pastoris expression system and FPLC/SEC; >90% purity confirmed by SDS-PAGE.
  • Generated site-directed mutagenesis constructs and performed in vitro binding and cleavage assays to map functional interaction domains.
  • Generated dual-species RNA-seq datasets from macrophage–Candida infection models; contributed to pathway enrichment and differential expression analysis.
  • Executed in vivo virulence studies in murine models.
  • Provided evidence for a proteolytic layer regulating carbon metabolic flux under stress — distinct from transcriptional control alone.
I investigated how GPI-anchored aspartyl proteases regulate metabolic state transitions in Candida glabrata under host-mimicking stress. Focusing on CgYps1, I characterised its interaction with glycolytic and pentose phosphate pathway enzymes using protein purification, mutagenesis, and cleavage assays. These findings point toward a proteolytic regulatory layer over carbon flux — a mechanism distinct from transcriptional adaptation.
Metabolic adaptation in Candida glabrata may be regulated at the level of proteolytic control, not just transcriptional change.

CRISPR/Cas9-mediated knockout of PDS in Solanum melongena

Can CRISPR/Cas9 be validated as an effective gene editing approach for the PDS locus in brinjal via Agrobacterium-mediated transformation?

Detail↓
  • Designed a CRISPR/Cas9 knockout vector targeting the PDS (Phytoene desaturase) gene in Solanum melongena L.
  • Validated CRISPR/Cas9 editing efficacy via Agrobacterium-mediated plant transformation.
Working under Dr. Radhika Tippani, this project applied CRISPR/Cas9 methodology to a plant system, building foundational competency in molecular cloning, vector design, and plant transformation workflows.
