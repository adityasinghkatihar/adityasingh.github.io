Who I am (and why it matters)

I’m a mechanism-first biologist who wants to translate biological decision-making into formal computational logic.

Research identity

Working with Candida glabrata taught me to treat biology as an adaptive system with agency. I’m now focused on formalizing how environmental pressure maps to strategy shifts—metabolically and proteolytically— and how that logic can be modeled for prediction and hypothesis generation.

Pros (what you can trust)

Rapid assimilation of new techniques Cross-disciplinary adaptability (wet lab → computational) Data-driven hypothesis refinement Mechanistic reasoning across biological scales Strong experimental design under complex conditions

Growth areas (where I’m leveling up)

Optimization of model interpretability in biological contexts Expanding mathematical intuition for dynamic systems Advanced ML model development (beyond classical ML) Translating biological problems into formal computational frameworks Building scalable and reusable data pipelines

Transition note (honest + focused)

I’m not moving into computation to “follow the trend.” I’m moving because the biological questions I keep facing (state choice under pressure, regulatory logic, and prediction from complex data) demand formal modeling—not only measurement.

Yapsin-linked metabolic adaptation

Host-like stress • Proteases • Metabolism

Tested protease role under host-like stress; optimized induction + purification + interaction assays; identified candidate metabolic interaction pattern.

Bench reasoning (what I learned)
  • Reasoned from metabolic burden to modify induction timing for improved expression/solubility.
  • Optimized solubilization before affinity purification to reduce non-specific expression.
  • Validated protein–protein interactions via immunoprecipitation and immuno-pulldown assays.
  • Used results to refine hypotheses about functional metabolic coupling in stress conditions.

Host–pathogen dataset building (CDFD)

Dual-species • Transcriptomics • Metabolomics

Contributed to generation of datasets for dual-species transcriptomic studies, integrating metabolomics approaches with targeted biochemical assays.

Why it matters for computation
  • Controls and measurement validity determine what patterns are even learnable.
  • Multi-modal integration requires careful feature mapping and quality checks.
  • Small protocol decisions change the biological signal we can model.
